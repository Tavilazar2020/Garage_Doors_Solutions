  Does your door make noise when it opens and closes?

The springs, the rollers, the hinges The door crashes at opening and closing.and the guide rails can scratch and make noise. This is specific to normal use and does not necessarily indicate a problem. Before you call a professional service technician, use a lubricant spray (especially recommended for garage doors). If after noise the noise persists, call a specialist garage operator for intervention.

  The Sectional Door Cable is worn.

Contact a person specializing in the installation and maintenance of the garage door. Do not attempt to remove, repair or adjust springs or cables. CAREFUL! High arc tension can cause serious injury or even death.

  Do the garage door or gate open hardly with a jerky move or with loud noises?

This may be due to the lack of maintenance of the garage door or the operator (the engine). Consult a person specializing in the installation and maintenance of garage doors and gate automation for a check.

  When you operate the remote control button, the garage door or gate does not open.

This could be caused by several problems. The easiest is that at some distance the garage door or gate does not work, so you may need to replace the batteries. Other more serious problems could be: the receiver has problems. Please call a specialist for help.

  The garage door or gate does not close completely, it returns back up and remains in an open position.

An object can interrupt the invisible barrier made by the photocell or engine sensors are wrong. Please call a specialist for help.

  You have noticed rust stains at the bottom of the garage door.

These rust spots can be caused by salt (or other solutions) used in the winter to melt ice and snow or splashes of another chemical agent used to clean/grease the lawn. Make sure you have cleaned the garage door with soap and water to remove any debris that may remain on the outside of your garage door.
